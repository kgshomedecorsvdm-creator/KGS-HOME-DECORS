# KGS Home Décors — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Tamil Nadu-wide online shoppers discovering KGS Home Décors through search or social media. Primary motivation: purchase premium home décor and furniture with delivery across Tamil Nadu. Secondary: physical Virudhachalam showroom visitors who browse online first. They use mobile phones predominantly (likely 70%+ mobile traffic), shop in Indian vernacular browsing patterns (WhatsApp-native, UPI-comfortable), and expect a trustworthy, premium-feeling storefront that isn't cold or intimidating.

### Brand Personality
**3 words**: Warm · Rooted · Confident

KGS is not trying to be a Chennai luxury brand. It's Virudhachalam's finest — a real local store that has earned its reputation and now serves all of Tamil Nadu. The brand should feel like the best sari shop in your city: high quality, personally curated, genuinely trustworthy, not imported minimalism.

Emotional goals: customers should feel **safe** (their money is in good hands), **inspired** (these products would look beautiful in my home), and **helped** (the brand feels like a knowledgeable friend, not a faceless store).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Tamil Nadu shoppers browse during morning/afternoon in naturally lit environments. Light feels familiar, trustworthy, and warm.
- **Palette**: Warm off-whites (#FAF8F4, #FDF9F3), near-black ink (#1A1A1A), and the existing warm gold/amber (#C97840, #C5A880) as accent. These are authentic — keep them. Avoid adding new colors.
- **Typography**: Replace Crimson Pro + Jost (both on the AI-defaults list) with **Gloock** (headings — warm editorial serif, print-quality weight) + **Manrope** (body — geometric humanist sans, reads clearly on mobile).
- **Visual feel**: An Indian home furnishing catalog printed on quality paper. Not glossy European luxury, not mass-market discount. Think: the kind of shop where the owner knows what they're selling.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid Scandinavian cold minimalism, neon-accent dark mode, gradient-heavy flashiness.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over polish** — Every element should feel handpicked, not templated. Break the identical-card-grid habit.
2. **Mobile-first ordering** — The primary flow is discover → cart → WhatsApp confirmation → order. Every friction point in this path must be eliminated.
3. **Trust through specificity** — Real address, real hours, real WhatsApp number. Show the store, not stock photography. Specifics build trust more than generic badges.
4. **Restraint in decoration** — The products are the decoration. The UI should frame them, not compete with them.
5. **India-first interactions** — WhatsApp is the service layer. UPI is the payment. Design around these native behaviors, don't apologize for them.
